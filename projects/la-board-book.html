City Year Los Angeles board book

City Year L.A. needed a comprehensive document that their influential board members could refer to throughout the service year. This book first provides information about the organization's national scope, and then hones in on City Year L.A.’s local service, impact, events, and relevant contact information.

As the design lead, it felt at first like I was creating a discombobulated compilation scrapbookâ€”pushing together pieces from many existing documents. After getting the bones of the content together, however, we were able to better organize the information and remove repetitive content.

Creating consistent typographic rules and using an underlying grid helped to pull the 48-page book together into a useful document that got rave reviews, and was later provided as a starting template for other sites to create similar pieces.

Role

Layout design

Tools

InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop

Duration

Fall 2018

Each section of the book was assigned a different brand color (used in the upper left triangular section header, a thin strip on the right side of the spread, and in text that needed visual differentiation).

Many of the sections were statistics-heavy to demonstrate the value of the organization. Depending on the context, I paired icons from our icon library with the selected data points.
