Museum of Pop Culture 2021 annual report

The 2021 annual report was a challenge on three fronts: (1) a tight timeline, as work had not begun even halfway through the year, (2) a large number of collaborators and people weighing in on content, and (3) the sheer amount of content that had to be included in 20 pages.

﹢Challenge 1: A tight timeline

We separated the report into two separate workflows: layout design and copy. While I believe that having content worked out in advance helps the designer to visually communicate the information being presented, we worked collaboratively on the structure up front, establishing the types of information that I would be designing and the system the report would live in.

By establishing a system first and using fairly strict word count requirements per section (initially using lorem ipsum), we were able to fairly seamlessly progress on both design and copy.

An evolution of a spread, starting with placeholder copy. Note the evolution of column structure, headline treatment, subhead treatment, and the introduction of the large panel on the left to mark the beginnng of a new section.

﹢Challenge 2: Tons of input

The content leads took ownership of communicating with the many, many stakeholders to make sure they had eyes on the content before it went to print. The process broke down into many rounds of edits, sometimes broken up into smaller sections to expedite the process. Some edits were quick, actionable copyedits, while larger questions on structure, order, or stylistic pieces that ran throughout the publication tended to be larger conversations with the team.

To make a table of numbers more understandable, I added a visualization. While past reports broke down Total Revenues and Contributed Revenues, I wasn't sure what story that was telling. What is the adueince looking for? What is the story we want to communicate?

Instead of two versions of Revenues, I thought it was more important to communicate income vs. spending — answering the question, how is MoPOP spending its money?

﹢Challenge 3: The museum did a LOT in 2021

The 2020 annual report was 60 pages long, and one of the goals of the 2021 report was to majorly condense it (both for print cost and for it to be more usable in Advancement meetings). While I love the inclusion of beautiful, full-spread photography in pieces like this, the constraints were such that I had to make peace with less negative space.
